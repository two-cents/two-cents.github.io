Two Reasons for Two Cents

Feedback

The idea of asking 10 friends for feedback all at once was easier and less time consuming than asking them separately.

Other Slack groups were too large and self-serving

Dribbble wasn’t being used for feedback like it used to.

There was a need for a small community of trusted eyes.

Friendship

Conferences and workshops are great, but sometimes you miss out on that community until the next year. We wanted to create a place where community was rooted in in-person experiences, but continued to grow during times that we were apart. In-house or freelance work can get lonely but knowing there are other people right there with you can offer comfort in sharing struggles and victories.

Beginning Steps

  1. Set up a Slack Workspace for your Two Cents Chapter
  2. Recruit 10-12 members
  3. Create new channels as needed
  4. Plan a retreat

Organizing Your Slack Channels

Our current mash of channels is as follows but may change if a new topic arises

  • #feedback — post work in progress shots to be reviewed by fellow members
  • #fire — work we find by other artists, designers, illustrators
  • #gaming — video game news/discussions
  • #general — used for announcements, posting other members’ work to share, general discussion
  • #jobs — potential leads or job news
  • #movies-and-tv — movies and tv talk, if there is a movie you would like to talk about specifically with other people who have seen it, another channel is created to avoid spoilers
  • #prayer — safe place to share prayer requests and praises
  • #random — weird discussions, fun finds, not “general”
  • #retreat — discussions on details regarding future retreats

Planning A Retreat

Google hangouts are great (and encouraged!) but planning a retreat to spend time with each other in person is the best. Plan trips around activities and locations all members can get into!

FAQs

Can I join the original Two Cents chapter?

No. And not because we’re mean or exclusive but because the magic of a small community is that it is capped at a certain number. We’ve learned that the smaller, the better. There are plenty of talented people out there waiting to be a part of your group.
